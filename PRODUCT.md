# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Jilin University (JLU) students in Changchun, browsing on phones between classes or planning group meals. They want to quickly find good, affordable food near campus or across the city. Secondary: Changchun locals and visitors looking for restaurant recommendations.

## Product Purpose

An interactive food map for Changchun that lets users discover, search, filter, and contribute restaurant information. Success: students open it before every meal decision. It becomes the go-to local food resource for the JLU community.

## Brand Personality

Warm, vibrant, youthful. Feels like a friend's recommendation, not a corporate directory. Energetic without being childish. Confident in its local knowledge.

## Anti-references

- Cluttered old-school review sites (early Dianping, Yelp circa 2015): too much information density, no visual hierarchy
- Generic SaaS dashboards: too corporate, too sterile for a food discovery product
- Over-gamified apps: excessive badges, points, leaderboards that distract from finding food
- Bland government-style information portals: lifeless typography, institutional color palettes

## Design Principles

1. **Food first**: every screen leads to a restaurant. Navigation never obscures discovery.
2. **One-thumb friendly**: primary actions reachable with one hand on a phone screen.
3. **Local flavor**: the interface should feel like it belongs to Changchun, not a generic template.
4. **Trust through clarity**: ratings, prices, and distances are always scannable at a glance.
5. **Low friction contribution**: adding a restaurant should feel as easy as sending a message.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Sufficient contrast ratios on dark backgrounds
- Touch targets minimum 44px for mobile
- Reduced motion support via prefers-reduced-motion
